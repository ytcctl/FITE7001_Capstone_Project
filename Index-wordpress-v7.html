TokenHub: Tokenizing HKSTP Startups

A permissioned blockchain platform concept integrated with the Hong Kong Science & Technology Parks (HKSTP) Investment Platform to issue compliant security tokens representing fractional equity in HKSTP portfolio startups, and to enable secondary trading with atomic Delivery-versus-Payment (DvP) settlement.

1) Overview

Executive summary

  • Digitize fundraising workflows using smart contracts to reduce delay and operational friction.
  • Offer fractional ownership of HKSTP-backed startups using compliant security tokens.
  • Support a regulated secondary market to improve liquidity for investors.
  • Enable instant / near-instant settlement through atomic DvP (assets and payment settle together).

What makes it “HKSTP-ready”

  • Institutional maturity: use HKSTP’s rigorous vetting and ecosystem support.
  • Compliance-first: permissioned transfer controls + KYC/AML.
  • Investor experience: a portal for onboarding, wallet management, trading, settlement, and reporting.
  • Auditability: immutable on-chain events enable cap-table accuracy and reporting automation.

2) Problem & Motivation

Startup pain points

  • Extended fundraising timelines (often months).
  • Manual workflows (cap-table updates, subscriptions, reporting).
  • High transaction and operational costs.
  • Slow fund transfer and settlement processes.

Investor pain points

  • Limited liquidity in private markets (hard to exit early).
  • Lack of fractional ownership reduces access and flexibility.
  • Restricted access (often limited to accredited/institutional investors).
  • Information asymmetry and sporadic reporting.

3) Proposed Solution: TokenHub

Token layer

One token per startup, Ethereum-compatible, with compliance-aware transfer logic.

  • Security tokens representing fractional startup equity.
  • ERC-3643 (T-REX) considered for built-in compliance and identity integration.
  • Internal “safe-list” for operational addresses (e.g., treasury/custody/escrow).

Compliance & approvals

“Policy off-chain, enforcement on-chain” using per-transaction approvals.

  • KYC/AML.
  • Portal checks suitability, lock-ups, concentration caps, jurisdiction rules.
  • Compliance Service issues one-time signed approval (attestation) bound to each transfer.

Trading & settlement

Off-chain matching, on-chain atomic settlement (DvP).

  • Order-book model for secondary trading.
  • Atomic DvP: security token + tokenized cash move in the same transaction.
  • Event-driven reporting: transfers and settlements feed analytics & audit trails.

6.1 Cold wallet vs. Hot wallet Management Architecture Design

6.1.1 Custodial Wallet Management Architecture: Hot vs. Cold Storage Protocols

To comply with the SFC’s updated conduct standards for Virtual Asset Trading Platforms (VATPs) issued on January 16, 2025, TokenHub implements a tiered storage architecture that prioritizes the security of client assets while maintaining operational liquidity.

6.1.2 The “98/2” Asset Allocation Requirement

TokenHub adheres to the mandatory 98/2 Requirement, ensuring that at least 98% of client virtual assets are stored in cold storage (offline), with no more than 2% held in hot storage (online) for daily transaction processing.

Hot WalletAlways Online< 2%Instant withdrawals, FPS/tokenized deposit settlement.Real-time monitoring, IP whitelisting, HSM-protected hot keys.
Warm WalletPartially OnlineTransientBuffer for rebalancing; daily transactional sweeps.Multi-signature (2-of-3) requirement for manual approval of flows.

6.1.3 Cold Storage Security Standards

TokenHub’s cold storage infrastructure incorporates rigorous safeguards to meet the SFC’s Expected Standards L and M, and the CryptoCurrency Security Standard (CCSS) Level 3 benchmarks.

Hardware Security Module (HSM) Certification
  • FIPS 140-2 Level 3+: The HSM must possess a minimum certification of FIPS 140-2 Level 3, providing high resistance to physical tampering and identity-based authentication.
  • Tamper-Active Protection: For high-value startup tokens, TokenHub utilizes Level 4 HSMs, which detect mechanical, chemical, or environmental attacks and instantly zeroize all sensitive cryptographic parameters before compromise.
  • No Smart Contracts: The cold wallet system is explicitly prohibited from using smart contracts for internal custody logic.

6.1.4 Air-Gapped Signing Workflow

  • Step 1: Construction: An unsigned transaction is built on a “watch-only” online terminal.
  • Step 2: Unidirectional Transfer: The unsigned transaction is transferred to the air-gapped device via animated QR codes or a quarantined USB drive.
  • Step 3: Offline Signing: The transaction is reviewed and signed on the air-gapped machine. The private key never leaves this isolated environment.
  • Step 4: Return & Broadcast: The signed bundle is returned to the online terminal and broadcast to the blockchain.

6.1.5 Operational Flow and Rebalancing

The platform maintains a real-time monitoring system to enforce the 98/2 ratio. Upon receipt of client assets in hot storage, the system automatically triggers a real-time sweep to move excess funds into cold storage. Small withdrawals are serviced instantly from hot storage.

6.2 Permissioned Custody and Transfer

Under the SFC’s guidelines, tokenized assets cannot be managed through a purely “wallet-to-wallet” or self-custody approach typical of public DeFi protocols. Instead, TokenHub utilizes a “Permissioned Custody” model.

6.2.1 The Permissioned Ledger State Machine

The ledger does not allow unrestricted transfers. Every transaction is intercepted by a compliance layer that validates the identity and eligibility of both the sender and the receiver.

Component Implementation Regulatory Goal
Token ContractERC-3643 (T-REX)Standardizes transfer logic with compliance hooks.
Identity RegistryONCHAINID MappingEnsures all participants are KYC/AML verified.
Compliance ContractModular Rule SetsEnforces jurisdictional limits, investor caps, and lock-ups.

The interaction flow for a standard transfer:

  1. Initiation: A participant initiates a transfer(to, amount) call.
  2. Eligibility Check: The token contract calls the IdentityRegistry to verify the receiver.
  3. Compliance Check: The token contract calls the Compliance contract to verify offering rules.
  4. Execution: If all checks pass, the ledger is updated. Otherwise it reverts with a compliance error.

6.2.2 The forcedTransfer() Function

The forcedTransfer() function allows a licensed custodian or platform administrator to reallocate tokens in response to a court order, liquidator instruction, or regulatory seizure.

6.3 Managing Identity with ONCHAINID

TokenHub uses ONCHAINID (ERC-734/735) to manage identities without exposing personal data on-chain.

6.3.1 Minting and Recovery

Tokens are minted/burned only by licensed custodians. If an investor loses their private key, the custodian can burn the tokens in the lost wallet and mint replacements to a verified new wallet.

6.3.2 Deposit/Withdrawal Workflow via FPS

TokenHub integrates Hong Kong’s FPS and Project Ensemble to support instant deposit, withdrawal, and atomic DvP settlement.

7) Regulatory Framework / KYC / AML Click to expand ▼

7.1 Legal Characterization of Tokenized HKSTP Startups

Hong Kong’s existing legal and regulatory framework, primarily the Securities and Futures Ordinance (Cap. 571), serves as a foundation for classifying tokenized assets as “securities”. The framework adequately accommodates the issuance of tokenized shares in HKSTP startups on our TokenHub platform.

As highlighted in the SFC tokenization circular in November 2023, the “see-through” approach views tokenized securities as traditional securities with a tokenization wrapper.

7.2 SFC Type 1/7/VATP Licensing Strategy

Our TokenHub platform requires SFC Type 1 regulated activity (dealing in securities) and Type 7 regulated activity (providing automated trading services).

The SFC strongly suggests that virtual asset trading platforms apply for licences under both the SFO and AMLO regimes (VATP), ensuring comprehensive coverage.

7.3 Regulatory Analysis on Settlement Finality

Settlement finality refers to the discharge of an obligation that has become irrevocable. TokenHub relies on contractual finality through agreements embedded in platform terms and smart contract logic.

Impact on Smart Contract and Architecture Design:
  • Defining the Point of Finality: Settlement finality = predefined number of block confirmations (N blocks).
  • “Rectifiable Ledger” Architecture: The forcedTransfer() function allows the ledger to synchronize with legal finality determined by a court.
  • Atomic Settlement (DvP): Assets transfer and payment settlement occur as a single, indivisible transaction.

7.4 Regulatory Analysis on Custody Arrangements

Unlike purely virtual assets (like Bitcoin), tokenized HKSTP startups are legally characterized as “securities”. Hence, custody arrangements must comply with SFC’s regulations. The architecture employs a “Permissioned Custody” model.
